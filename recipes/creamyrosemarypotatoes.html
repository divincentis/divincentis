Creamy Rosemary Potatoes 🥔

Prep Time: 20 mins | Cook Time: 1 hr | Yield: 9 servings

The most amped-up version of potatoes au gratin in existence — made extra decadent with butter, cream cheese, half-and-half, and cream. So good you might have to go to confession after the last bite, even if you're not Catholic.

Notes

  • Don't undersalt — there's a lot of dairy and starch to season. Taste the cream sauce before pouring it over the potatoes, and don't forget to season the potatoes themselves.
  • A mandoline makes quick work of slicing the potatoes evenly. Aim for slices about 3mm (⅛ inch) thick.
  • Make sure the cream cheese is fully at room temperature before adding it to the skillet — cold cream cheese will leave lumps in the sauce.
  • The dish can be assembled a few hours ahead and refrigerated; add 10-15 minutes to the bake time if going in cold.
  • Leftovers reheat well at 165°C (325°F) covered with foil for about 20 minutes.
