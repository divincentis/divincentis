Notes

  • While traditional Italian taralli are often boiled before baking, this family recipe skips that step - going straight to baking for a simpler preparation.
  • The original recipe calls for "Spry" - a brand of vegetable shortening popular in mid-20th century American kitchens. You can substitute any vegetable oil or olive oil.
  • The dough should be firm enough to roll but not sticky. Add flour or liquid as needed to achieve the right consistency.
  • These keep well in an airtight container for several weeks.
  • Perfect served with beer, wine, or as part of an antipasto platter.
  • Some families make a sweeter version without pepper - this savory version is specifically meant to pair with drinks!
