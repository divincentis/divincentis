Coffee via Aeropress

The way we do it, anyway.
Prep Time: 5 mins | Yield: 1 cup | Ratio: 15:1 water:coffee

Aeropress

The following process is a basic recipe that reliably produces great coffee with an Aeropress. Typical water:coffee ratio is 15:1.

Equipment Needed

  • Aeropress
  • Kettle
  • Scale
  • Grinder
  • Coffee mug

Instructions

  1. Start heating filtered water in kettle. 175°F - 185°F is a good temperature range.
  2. Place filter into Aeropress.
  3. Set coffee cup on scale and place Aeropress onto coffee cup.
  4. Measure beans using Aeropress as a vessel. 20 grams is a good starting point.
  5. Dump beans into grinder and put Aeropress back onto the coffee mug.
  6. By now your kettle should be at least warm. Pour 20-30 mL water into the empty Aeropress to rinse the filter and preheat your mug.
  7. Grind beans. A drop or two of water on the unground beans reduces static in the ground coffee.
  8. Shake excess water out of Aeropress and dump water out of coffee mug.
  9. Place the Aeropress and mug back on the scale.
  10. Dump freshly ground beans into Aeropress, tare the scale.
  11. Pour 150 mL heated water from the kettle into the Aeropress. Remove mug from scale.
  12. Stir for 30 seconds using the Aeropress paddle. Don't whirlpool.
  13. Press plunger into Aeropress to slowly filter coffee. Should take 20 seconds or so to get all the coffee through.
  14. Place mug back on scale. Tare scale.
  15. Pour another 150 mL heated water from kettle directly into mug. Coffee is done!
  16. Over sink, press plunger tightly against grounds to squeeze out excess water.
  17. Remove filter cap, rinse, and place on drying rack.
  18. Over trash can, push the plunger into the Aeropress to eject filter and grounds.
  19. Rinse plunger end of Aeropress assembly, pull plunger out, and place both parts on drying rack.
  20. Enjoy your coffee!
